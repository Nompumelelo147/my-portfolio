My practice sits at the intersection of public affairs, policy communications, and high-stakes reputation management — serving organisations whose decisions shape industries and whose words carry institutional weight.

I have led the communications strategy behind South Africa's most significant mining regulatory reform in two decades — the Minerals Resources and Development Bill — managing CEO media briefings, coordinating parliamentary submission messaging, and sustaining a national narrative through an extended, contested policy process. At Mining Indaba across three successive cycles, I have delivered 169+ media placements and R10M+ in AVE while managing real-time digital operations for Africa's premier investment conference.

My client portfolio spans Minerals Council South Africa, Eskom, PPS Insurance, FNB, and Cubespace — organisations where the margin for error is narrow and the audience is always scrutinising. My academic foundation in Public Relations (University of Johannesburg) is sharpened by studies toward an LLB at UNISA, giving me a precise lens for reading regulatory risk, legislative process, and policy environment in ways most communications practitioners cannot.

Challenge
Shift public understanding of mining's economic contribution — reaching both national media audiences and local communities, in their own languages, with credibility and clarity.

A two-phase national campaign — public-facing, then employee-focused — anchored by evidence-led fact sheets and a multilingual community radio broadcast strategy across five languages.
